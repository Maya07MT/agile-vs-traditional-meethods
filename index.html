Introduction

Software development can be a particularly complex sector, especially when it comes to large applications and work teams. Starting to develop a product without a clear methodology will lead to an even more complex process, which will lead to problems and, ultimately, a bad end result. In recent decades, agile software development methodologies have taken over, as the latest Project Manager Institute (PMI) study shows, 71% of software engineering companies use these methodologies. Some companies continue to use traditional software development methodologies, which may remain valid depending on the project and the company. In this research we will learn more about these methodologies

Agile methodology

Agile management has a much more flexible and adaptable work process, focused on solving problems, rather than following a rigorous process. So each project is divided into small parts that have to be completed and delivered in a few weeks. The aim is to develop quality products and services that respond to the needs of customers whose priorities change at an increasing speed.

The main advantages of agile are:

1. Improves quality: Minimizes delivery errors and improves customer experience and functionality.
2. Increased commitment: Improves employee satisfaction and generates team awareness.
3. Speed: Shortens production cycles and minimizes reaction and decision-making times.
4. Increased productivity: By better allocating resources, and more dynamically, it improves production according to the priorities of the company.

Disadvantages of agile are:

1. At the beginning of the project, it is difficult to accurately determine the amount of time and money that will be needed to complete it, due to the constantly changing requirements.
2. The team needs to have a solid foundation and skills.
3. A high level of interaction between the client and the developers is required.
4. Lack of attention to documentation can make it difficult for new team members to access it.

Traditional methodology

Emphasis is placed on the total planning of all the work to be done and once everything is detailed, the software product development cycle begins. They focus especially on process control, through a rigorous definition of roles, activities, artifacts, tools and notations for modeling and detailed documentation. They are not appropriate methods when working in an environment where requirements cannot be predicted or may vary.

The main advantages of traditional are:

1. They focus on keeping a thorough documentation of the entire project.
2. They have a project plan.
3. Stages are clearly defined by defining roles.

The main disadvantages of traditional are:

1. High costs when implementing a change.
2. It executes the stages only once, what is defined in each stage is immovable and until it does not successfully finish one stage is not passed to the next.
3. The user does not see the product until the end.

Differences: Level of flexibility

Traditional management is a rigorous process where, once the plan is completed, the manager is responsible for communicating it to the team and ensuring that each member implements the plan according to their needs. By contrast, Agile offers much more flexibility when making changes, as members are free to exchange ideas to help further improve the product. Agile methods focus more on creating the right products than on monitoring a rigid structure.

Ownership and transparency

In traditional project management, ownership belongs to the project manager. Therefore, he is responsible for planning and documenting the product journey, without customer involvement. Meanwhile, team members often say nothing about the results of their efforts or the progress of the project. In the agile approach, the project is a team. They all work together to develop a plan and monitor product progress from start to finish. Such transparency plays a key role in maintaining a productive and highly cohesive work environment.

Problem solving

In case of unexpected failure, team members should report the problem to the manager. Under an agile approach, they are empowered to make their own decisions and solve the problem internally, without wasting time unnecessarily. By initiating and approaching the process, ideas help them to solve most of the problems that hinder progress and therefore rarely pose minor or insignificant problems to their managers, unless extreme decisions are made.

Feedback

In the traditional approach, each process is clearly defined and planned from the beginning of the project. The project must be completed within the estimated time and budget. Therefore, any major changes or comments that might move the deadline are omitted. While agile management allows constant feedback, this will help us achieve a better result.

Conclusion

A traditional management of the new market is a rigid and little adaptable process, since it focuses on leadership and unidirectional communication. On the other hand, agile management has a much more flexible and adaptable work process, focused on solving problems, rather than following a rigorous process, in addition to enhancing assertive and communicative leadership.
